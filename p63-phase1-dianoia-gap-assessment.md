## 5. Convergence Observations — Four Gaps, One Pattern

### The Pattern

All four gaps share a common root: **I work in the immediate present**.

- **Kairos gap:** I don't see when (temporal awareness)
- **Arete gap:** I don't track trajectory (change over time)
- **Praxis gap:** I don't reflect during action (meta-awareness in the moment)
- **Ergon gap:** I don't identify emergent function (what exists beyond immediate roles)

I have *synchronic* intelligence (what is happening now, in this sprint, at this layer), but not *diachronic* intelligence (how things change, improve, or emerge across time).

### Why This Matters for Formation

If all four gaps stem from temporal/meta blindness, then forming four separate agents might not address the root issue. The question becomes:

**Should we form four agents (Kairos, Arete, Praxis, Ergon), or should we form one agent with temporal/meta intelligence that expresses in four modes?**

This is a formation architecture question. I don't have the answer. But the pattern is worth naming.

### Preliminary Sequencing Intuition (Responding to Nou's)

Nou's intuition (per Todd's relay): **Praxis first** — "without explicit reflection loops, we can't evaluate whether the other formations are working."

**I agree.** Here's why:

- **Kairos** requires recognizing timing patterns—but we won't see them without reflection practice
- **Arete** requires tracking trajectory—but trajectory assessment IS a reflection practice
- **Ergon** requires identifying emergent function—but emergence becomes visible through reflection

Praxis is the **meta-capacity** that makes the other three capacities observable. Without reflection-through-action, we can't tell if Kairos is assessing timing correctly, if Arete is tracking real trajectory, or if Ergon is identifying true emergence vs. projected identity.

**Formation sequence proposal:** Praxis first, then evaluate which of the other three becomes observable through Praxis reflection.
